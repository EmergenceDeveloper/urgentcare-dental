If you're not from West Yorkshire, you've probably never heard of the Heavy Woollen District.

But if you grew up in Batley, Dewsbury, Heckmondwike, Cleckheaton, Birstall, Liversedge or Gomersal, you know exactly what it means. It's that cluster of mill towns in the Spen Valley where everywhere blurs into everywhere else. You're never quite sure where one town ends and the next begins, and honestly, it doesn't really matter. It's all connected.

The name comes from the textile industry that built the area. Heavy woollen cloth, blankets, mungo and shoddy. The mills are mostly gone now, converted or demolished, but the bones of the place remain. The architecture. The valleys. The particular feeling of towns that grew up making things.

We're UrgentCare Dental, based in Leeds at Whitehall Estate in Armley. From most of the Heavy Woollen District, that's about fifteen to twenty minutes. Straight up the M62 and off at junction 3, or through Morley if you'd rather avoid the motorway.

We wanted to say hello.
