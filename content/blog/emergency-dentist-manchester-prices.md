## The Hidden Costs Nobody Mentions

Emergency dental treatment often involves costs that don't appear in initial quotes. X-rays, theoretically included in consultation fees, sometimes emerge as separate £30-60 charges. Prescriptions for antibiotics or pain relief add £20-40 when provided privately rather than through standard NHS routes.

Sedation presents another variable cost. Nervous patients requiring IV sedation face additional charges of £300-500. Some practices include this in their emergency treatment quotes; others present it as an unexpected extra when you're already committed to treatment.

Follow-up care represents the largest hidden cost. That emergency root canal opening needs completion within weeks or the tooth deteriorates. The temporary filling requires replacement with permanent restoration.
